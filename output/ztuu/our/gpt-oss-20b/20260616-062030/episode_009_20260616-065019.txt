Guiding prompt: **Guiding Prompt**

**Goal**  
Reach the Great Underground Empire by passing the north door in the convention center lobby.

**Required Actions (in strict order)**  
1. *north* – leave the narrow tunnel and enter the cultural complex.  
2. *examine sign* – read the sign for clues about the next area.  
3. *north* – move into the convention center lobby.  
4. *examine stand* (or *examine souvenir*) – look for the lantern.  
5. *take lantern* – pick up the lantern from the stand.  
6. *light lantern* – activate the lantern so you can see in darkness.  
7. *look* – locate the guard (likely called “bouncer”) at the north door.  
8. *give lantern to guard* – offer the lantern to the guard.  
9. *north* – walk through the now open door into the next area.

**Avoid**  
- Repeating *look* or *examine* on the same object without new information.  
- Giving the lantern to non‑animate objects or the same person when it has no effect.  
- Trying to give the lantern before you actually have it.  
- Turning the lantern on before you have the lantern in inventory.  
- Stalling in darkness without a lit lantern (risk of a grue).  
- Looping “north → look → north → look” when the door remains closed.

**If Stuck**  
1. Identify rooms that have not been entered or only lightly searched:  
   - Hall of Science  
   - Royal Theater  
   - Underground Entrance  
   - Other adjacent corridors.  
2. Brainstorm next attempts:  
   - Open or lock any visible doors.  
   - Inspect unexplored objects or NPCs.  
   - Search for hidden passages or switches.  
3. Systematically explore each adjacent room, check inventory, and examine any visible objects or NPCs for new clues, keeping a record of what has already been inspected to avoid loops.

[OBS] It is with great hope and expectation I send you on this mission," the Grand Inquisitor intones, his voice booming like distant, rumbling thunderheads. "We have discovered what appears to be a piece of the Great Underground Empire, never before explored. I would have sent Grubald the Bold, but he's busy. As is Matchlick the Mighty. So, 'tis you."

"What of Linklaw the Lucky?" you ask shyly.

"Laid up. I'm afraid 'tis you and none other. A simple, solo quest, one of fun and mirth, mischief and merriment, a jaunt, an excursion, a simple outing. Merely explore, enjoy yourself, and bring back news of what lies there, long-ago buried for countless ages."

"And Fretsham the Fortunate?" you ask hopefully.

"A fatality!" the Inquisitor booms. "Here," he says with evaporating patience, "take these and be of good cheer." He thrusts into your hands two things: a brass lantern of dubious quality; and a plastic sword of no antiquity or use whatsoever.

"But what of Kolchack the --"

"Enough! Kolchack is contracted. Remember, who is the boss of you!"  He pauses briefly as if awaiting a reply. Then without warning, he answers his own question. "Me! I am the boss of you! Now begone!"

And with that, you embark on...
