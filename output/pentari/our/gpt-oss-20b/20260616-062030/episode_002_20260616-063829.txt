4. **Search for hidden compartments** –  
   * Search the dresser (including its base and hidden panels).  
   * Search the bed (under the mattress, in the headboard).  
   * Search the chest (if it is a separate object).  
   * Search the window, floor, and wall for any hidden mechanisms or passages.  
5. **Attempt to open the door** – if it is unlocked, open it; if it is locked, try any key you found.  
6. **If the door remains inaccessible** –  
   * List all lightly‑searched objects and rooms (door, dresser, bed, chest, window, floor, wall, hallway, courtyard, barracks, armory).  
   * Brainstorm possible next attempts (e.g., "search dresser base", "search window sill", "search floor tiles", "look for a hidden lever").  
   * Systematically explore each remaining object or room in turn, noting any new clues.  
7. **Avoid**:  
   * Repeating the same command that produced “you can't see any such thing” or “you find nothing of interest.”  
   * Unproductive loops such as endless “look”, “search bed”, or “open drawer1” after failure.  
   * Commands that do not change the state or are not explicitly useful for progression.
